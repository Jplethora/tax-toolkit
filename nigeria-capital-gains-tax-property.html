Property gains

Capital gains questions around Nigerian property, simplified.

When property is sold, owners often want to know what part of the gain matters, what paperwork should exist, and why old records suddenly become important.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Capital gains conversations usually show up at the point of sale, but the best preparation starts much earlier. Purchase records, improvement records, and transaction evidence all shape how clearly the position can be reviewed.

Documents owners should try to keep

  • Purchase agreements and proof of acquisition cost.
  • Evidence of major improvements that affected the asset.
  • Sale documentation and transfer records.
  • Professional correspondence where the transaction is complex.

Why this matters for diaspora owners

Owners living abroad may not be physically close to the records they need. That makes early organization and trusted documentation even more important.

Keep perspective: The tax issue is usually easier to review when the transaction trail is complete.
