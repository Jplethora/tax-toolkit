Nigeria Capital Gains Tax on Property

Understanding tax considerations when selling property in Nigeria

Introduction

Capital Gains Tax (CGT) is a tax that may apply when certain assets are sold and a gain is realized. In Nigeria, capital gains tax rules can apply to the disposal of assets such as land, buildings, and other forms of property.

Individuals selling property in Nigeria often want to understand whether tax may apply and how the rules work in practice.

---

What Is Capital Gains Tax?

Capital Gains Tax is generally associated with the profit realized when an asset is sold at a value higher than the price at which it was acquired.

In Nigeria, capital gains tax rules are contained in the Capital Gains Tax Act and apply to certain types of asset disposals.

---

Does Capital Gains Tax Apply to Property Sales?

Property transactions can sometimes fall within the scope of capital gains tax rules depending on the nature of the transaction and applicable exemptions.

Common property transactions that may raise tax questions include:

  • Selling land
  • Selling residential property
  • Selling investment property
  • Transferring property ownership
---

Factors That May Affect Tax Treatment

The tax treatment of property transactions may depend on various factors including:

  • The original acquisition cost of the property
  • The value at which the property is sold
  • The type of property involved
  • Applicable exemptions under Nigerian tax law
  • The individual's tax residency status
---

Property Transactions for Nigerians in the Diaspora

Many Nigerians living abroad invest in property within Nigeria. These transactions may involve additional considerations related to residency, cross-border income, and documentation requirements.

Diaspora Nigerians selling property may wish to understand the relevant tax rules and reporting expectations.

Importance of Proper Documentation

Maintaining proper documentation is often important for property transactions. Records that may be relevant include:

  • Property purchase agreements
  • Land registry documentation
  • Evidence of acquisition cost
  • Sale agreements
---
Important Note

Tax rules relating to property transactions can vary depending on individual circumstances and legal requirements. Individuals involved in property transactions may wish to seek professional advice when evaluating potential tax implications.
