Is There Tax on Money Sent to Nigeria?

Millions of Nigerians living abroad send money home every year to support family members, invest in businesses, or purchase property.

One common question is whether these remittances are taxed in Nigeria.

1. Personal Remittances

Money sent by Nigerians abroad to family members for personal support is generally considered personal transfers rather than taxable income.

Examples include:

  • Family support payments
  • Education support
  • Medical support
  • Personal gifts

2. Business-Related Transfers

If funds sent to Nigeria are connected to business activities or investment income, different tax considerations may apply.

Examples include:

  • Investment capital
  • Business funding
  • Profit distributions

3. Record Keeping

Diaspora individuals may benefit from maintaining proper documentation for financial transfers, especially where funds are connected to property or investments.
