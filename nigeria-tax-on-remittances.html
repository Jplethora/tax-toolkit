Remittances

How to think about remittances and Nigerian tax questions.

Sending money into Nigeria is common, but the tax question depends heavily on why the money is moving and what sits behind it.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Remittances are often family support transfers, investment funding, or movement of personal savings. The key issue is usually not the transfer itself, but whether the money reflects taxable income, business activity, or asset movement that should be understood properly.

Questions worth asking first

  • Is the money simply family support or a gift?
  • Is it connected to a business, investment, or property activity in Nigeria?
  • Will the recipient use it in a way that creates taxable income locally?
  • Do records clearly show the source and purpose of the transfer?

Why context matters

Two transfers of the same amount can have very different tax implications depending on their purpose. That is why clear paperwork and a basic understanding of the transaction are important.

Helpful habit: Keep short notes, transfer confirmations, and purpose records, especially when funds support property or business activity.
