Is Money Sent to Nigeria Taxable?

The complete 2026 guide to remittances, gifts, and investment transfers for Nigerians abroad.

Jerisma Plethora Tax Advisory

Diaspora Tax & Compliance

Every year, Nigerians in the diaspora send over $20 billion home to support family, pay school fees, build houses, and invest in businesses. It's a lifeline for millions of families.

But a question we hear constantly from our clients in the US, UK, Canada and Europe is: "Will FIRS tax my family for the money I send? Do I need to declare it?"

At a Glance

  • ✓ Personal remittances (family support, gifts, medical, education) are NOT taxable in Nigeria.
  • ✓ Business or investment transfers are not taxed on arrival, but any profit they generate IS taxable.
  • ✓ Keep clear records and use licensed channels to avoid CBN & bank flags.

Personal Remittances: Family Support, Education, Medical & Gifts

Generally NOT taxable. This is the most important point for 95% of diaspora transfers.

Under Nigerian tax law (Personal Income Tax Act as amended by Finance Acts 2020-2024), the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) taxes income, profits, and gains – not gifts or family support. Money you send from your already-taxed income abroad to your parents, spouse, or siblings is classified as a personal remittance, not income to the receiver.

What is NOT taxed:

  • Monthly upkeep and family support
  • School fees, tuition, and education support
  • Medical bills and hospital payments
  • Gifts for weddings, birthdays, burials, or emergencies
  • Money to build a personal family house (not for rent)
  • Charitable donations to family or religious bodies

The Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) specifically encourages diaspora inflows through licensed International Money Transfer Operators (IMTOs) like WorldRemit, Remitly, Sendwave, Lemfi, and Naira payout. Your family receives the money in Naira, with no FIRS deduction.

Note for Senders: Nigeria won't tax you, but your country of residence might. For example, the US has an annual gift tax exclusion ($18,000 per person for 2024). The UK has inheritance tax rules on large gifts. Always check your local laws.

Business-Related Transfers: Investments, Funding & Profits

This is where tax considerations begin. The money itself isn't taxed when it lands, but what you do with it determines your tax.

Transfer Type Taxable on Arrival? Nigerian Tax Implication
Investment Capital No Get a Certificate of Capital Importation (CCI) from your bank. Future dividends & rental income are taxable.
Business Funding No Not income, but company profits are subject to 30% Company Income Tax + VAT.
Buying Property to Rent No Rental income is taxable at 10% WHT (for individuals). File annually.
Profit Repatriation (to you abroad) Yes (WHT) Dividends, interest, royalties sent abroad attract 10% withholding tax.

Key Rule: If the transfer is to generate income in Nigeria, you must register for tax. That means getting a TIN, filing returns, and paying tax on the profits – not the capital you sent.

Record Keeping for Diaspora

Nigerian banks are now required to report large and frequent inflows under AML/CFT rules. To protect yourself and your family, keep immaculate records:

  • 1Use only licensed channels. Avoid crypto P2P or unregistered agents for large sums. Always get a receipt with "Family Support" or "Investment Capital" as narration.
  • 2Keep transfer confirmations for 6 years. FIRS can ask for source of funds, especially for property purchases above ₦50m.
  • 3Get a Nigerian TIN if you invest. It's free and allows you file correctly. Without it, you can't get a CCI or Tax Clearance Certificate.
  • 4Separate accounts. Don't mix family upkeep money with business capital in the same Nigerian account.
  • 5Declare foreign income if you're resident. If you live in Nigeria >183 days and earn abroad, that income is taxable in Nigeria.
