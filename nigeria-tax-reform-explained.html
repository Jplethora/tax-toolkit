Nigeria Tax Reform Explained

Think of the reform as “one law, one ID, one portal.”

In one sentence

From 2026, all federal taxes live in one Act, your TIN works nationwide, and capital gains are taxed like salary.

What changes for you

Individuals

  • First ₦800,000 per year tax-free
  • Rent relief: claim 20% of rent paid (max ₦500k)
  • Selling land/house? Gain added to your income, taxed up to 25% (except your one-time main home)
  • Crypto, USD gains, side-hustle – now clearly taxable
  • File once a year by March 31 via phone

Small businesses

  • Turnover ≤₦50m? Pay 0% company income tax
  • Keep simple records; presumptive tax for informal traders
  • No more multiple levies – 4% development levy only if you grow big

Everyone

  • TIN = NIN + BVN. Get it free at tin.jtb.gov.ng
  • E-receipts matter for VAT claims
  • Late filing = automatic penalty

Why government did it

Broaden tax base, stop leakages, fund infrastructure without raising VAT.
