Tax rates

The Nigerian tax bands, explained without the noise.

Readers often hear the headline rate and miss the fact that personal income tax is layered across bands. This page keeps the explanation simple enough to follow.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Nigerian personal income tax works with progressive bands. That means different slices of taxable income are taxed at different rates instead of one flat rate applying to the entire amount.

Taxable sliceRate
First NGN 300,0007%
Next NGN 300,00011%
Next NGN 500,00015%
Next NGN 500,00019%
Next NGN 1,600,00021%
Above NGN 3,200,00024%

Why people misread these numbers

The most common mistake is assuming the highest band rate applies to the whole income figure. In a progressive structure, only the amount inside that band gets that rate.

What should come before the bands

In many practical examples, reliefs and deductions are considered before the tax bands are applied. That is why gross income and taxable income are not always the same thing.
