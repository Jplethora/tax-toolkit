Nigeria Tax Rates Explained (2026 Guide)

The old 7%–24% PIT bands are gone. Here’s the new, simplified 0%–25% structure, how the ₦800,000 exemption replaces CRA, and what it means for PAYE and self-assessment.

Jerisma Tax Advisory
• Updated 15 Jan 2026 • 7 min read

Introduction

Nigeria’s Personal Income Tax system has been completely overhauled under the Tax Reform Acts 2025. From 1 January 2026, the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) and State Internal Revenue Services will apply a new progressive tax table that prioritises low-income earners.

The biggest wins: the first ₦800,000 of your annual income is now tax-free, the Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA) has been abolished, and the top rate has been streamlined to 25%. This replaces the former six-band system of 7%, 11%, 15%, 19%, 21% and 24%.

Nigeria Personal Income Tax Bands 2026

Tax is calculated on your chargeable income — that is, gross income less statutory deductions (Pension, NHF, NHIS, and life assurance premiums). The rates are applied progressively:

Taxable Income Band Cumulative Income Rate
First ₦800,000 ₦0 – ₦800,000 0%
Next ₦2,200,000 ₦800,001 – ₦3,000,000 15%
Next ₦9,000,000 ₦3,000,001 – ₦12,000 18%
Next ₦13,000,000 ₦12,000,001 – ₦25,000,000 21%
Next ₦25,000,000 ₦25,000,001 – ₦50,000,000 23%
Above ₦50,000,000 ₦50,000,001 upwards 25%

Quick Example: ₦5,000,000 chargeable income

• First ₦800k @ 0% = ₦0   • Next ₦2.2m @ 15% = ₦330,000   • Balance ₦2m @ 18% = ₦360,000
Total Tax = ₦690,000 (13.8% effective rate) — down from ₦820,000 under the old bands.

Consolidated Relief Allowance: Now Replaced

CRA (₦200,000 + 20% of gross) is abolished

Under the 2026 Act, the complex CRA calculation has been removed completely. Instead, every individual automatically enjoys a ₦800,000 tax-free exemption. This is simpler, fairer, and more valuable for anyone earning below ₦5m annually. You no longer claim CRA on your tax form — your employer or SIRS will apply the ₦800k zero-band first.

PAYE vs Self-Assessment

For Employees (PAYE)

Your employer deducts tax monthly using the new bands. The ₦800k exemption is spread across 12 months (₦66,667/month tax-free). Ensure your December payslip shows the new rates. Annual PAYE returns remain due 31 January.

For Self-Employed & Business Owners

You file directly with your State IRS. Compute chargeable profit, deduct the ₦800k exemption, then apply the bands. 2026 returns are due by 31 March 2027. Same rates, same exemption — no CRA to calculate.

Minimum Tax Considerations

The 2026 reforms protect low earners but retain a minimum tax floor:

  • ✓Income ≤ ₦800,000: Fully exempt. You pay zero PIT.
  • !Income > ₦800,000: You pay the higher of (a) tax using the bands above, or (b) 1% of gross income after the ₦800k exemption. This prevents aggressive reliefs from reducing tax to zero.
  • iRent relief and other new personal reliefs are applied before the bands, not after.

Importance of Records in 2026

With CRA gone, FIRS will rely more on actual deductions and digital records. Keep these for at least 6 years:

Payslips & PAYE receipts
Pension, NHF, NHIS certificates
Rent receipts for rent relief
Business expenses & invoices

Pro tip: The new FIRS e-filing portal accepts scanned PDFs. Organise by tax year to speed up assessments.
