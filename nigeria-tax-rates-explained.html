Nigeria Tax Rates Explained

Understanding how personal income tax rates work in Nigeria

Introduction

Nigeria operates a progressive personal income tax system, meaning that tax rates increase as income rises. The system is designed so individuals with higher income levels contribute a larger percentage of their income in tax.

---

Nigeria Personal Income Tax Bands

The personal income tax system in Nigeria uses graduated tax bands. Each band applies a different tax rate to portions of income.

Income Band Tax Rate
First ₦300,000 7%
Next ₦300,000 11%
Next ₦500,000 15%
Next ₦500,000 19%
Next ₦1,600,000 21%
Above ₦3,200,000 24%
---

Consolidated Relief Allowance

Before calculating tax, individuals may be entitled to certain statutory reliefs. One commonly referenced relief is the Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA), which is designed to reduce taxable income before applying tax rates.

The application of reliefs may depend on individual circumstances and applicable regulations.

---

PAYE vs Self-Assessment

Employees usually have tax deducted automatically from their salary through the Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) system managed by their employer.

Self-employed individuals, freelancers, and business owners may be responsible for calculating their own taxes and filing returns with the relevant tax authority.

---

Minimum Tax Considerations

Even when taxable income is low or deductions apply, minimum tax rules may still apply in some situations. These rules are designed to ensure that individuals contribute a minimum amount of tax under the law.
