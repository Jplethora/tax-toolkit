SME tax

What small business owners in Nigeria should understand early.

For many SMEs, the tax problem is not a lack of effort but a lack of structure. This guide is built to lower that friction.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Small businesses sit at the intersection of growth pressure and compliance pressure. Founders are trying to sell, hire, and operate, while taxes often show up as a back-office issue until they become urgent.

Common tax areas SMEs hear about

  • Income tax on business profits or owner income.
  • VAT questions depending on the business activity.
  • Withholding tax awareness in certain transactions.
  • Record keeping strong enough to support filing and review.

What good record keeping usually includes

  • Sales records and invoices.
  • Expense receipts and supplier evidence.
  • Bank transaction history.
  • Basic profit tracking over the year.
Founder advantage: Businesses that keep cleaner books often solve tax issues faster and more cheaply than businesses that wait to reconstruct the year later.
