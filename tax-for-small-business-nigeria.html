Tax for Small Businesses in Nigeria

If you run a shop, online store, agency, or startup in Nigeria, the 2025 reform is good news: genuine small businesses pay 0% company income tax.

Are you “small”?

You qualify if BOTH:

  • Annual turnover ≤ ₦50 million
  • Fixed assets ≤ ₦250 million
  • Not in professional services (lawyers, accountants, etc. have different rules)

What you still pay

  • 0% CIT – but you must still file annual returns (nil or accounts)
  • Withholding Tax (WHT) deducted from your invoices – claim as credit
  • VAT – only if turnover > ₦50m; if below, register but charge 0% (voluntary registration allowed)
  • PAYE for staff – remit monthly by 10th
  • Development Levy 4% – NOT payable by small companies
  • Business premises levy, signage – state/local still apply

Filing checklist

  1. Get TIN via CAC (automatic at incorporation)
  2. Open TaxPro-Max account
  3. Keep records: sales invoices, expenses, bank statements, POS reports
  4. File annual income tax return within 6 months of year-end (even if 0% tax)
  5. File monthly PAYE and WHT remittances

Presumptive tax for informal traders

If you have no records, State IRS can assess fixed annual presumptive tax based on trade category (from 2026 framework). Keep simple cash book to avoid over-assessment.

Growth tip

Staying under ₦50m intentionally? The Act allows deduction of business expenses incurred up to 6 years before commencement – useful for startups. Once you cross threshold, CIT jumps to 30% and 4% levy applies.
