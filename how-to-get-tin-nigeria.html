How to Get a TIN in Nigeria

Your Tax Identification Number (TIN) is your single tax ID across Nigeria. Under the Nigeria Tax Act 2025, the TIN is now unified nationally and linked to your NIN, BVN, and CAC records.

Why you need a TIN

  • File annual returns and pay tax
  • Open business bank accounts
  • Register contracts, obtain permits, and access government services
  • Comply with FIRS and State IRS requirements

Three situations in 2026

  1. First-time registration – you have never had a TIN
  2. Retrieve existing TIN – JTB auto-generated one for you after NIN/BVN linkage
  3. Verify/activate – you have a CAC number but need to activate for tax

How to register (individuals)

  1. Go to tin.jtb.gov.ng (Joint Tax Board portal)
  2. Choose “Individual” → enter NIN and BVN, verify OTP
  3. Fill personal details exactly as on NIN
  4. Upload: valid ID, proof of address, passport photo
  5. Submit – approval typically 24–48 hours

Documents you need: NIN, BVN, phone/email, utility bill. Registration is free.

How to register (businesses)

  1. Incorporate with CAC first
  2. On JTB portal choose “Non-Individual” → enter CAC RC number
  3. Provide directors’ TINs/NINs, business address, nature of business
  4. Upload: CAC certificate, Form CAC 1.1, ID of directors
  5. FIRS automatically issues TIN; activate on TaxPro-Max

Other channels

  • FIRS or State IRS office (walk-in, free)
  • JTB mobile app
  • USSD (in some states)

Common issues

  • BVN-NIN mismatch – update at NIMC first
  • Duplicate TIN – request consolidation at JTB
  • CAC but no TIN – use “Verify TIN” then “activate”

Tip: Link your TIN before March 31 filing season to avoid delays. See deadline guide.
