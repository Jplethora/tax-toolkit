Nigeria Tax Reform 2025 Explained

On June 26, 2025, President Tinubu signed four Tax Reform Bills. Effective January 1, 2026, they replace 12+ old laws with a unified framework.

The four Acts

  1. Nigeria Tax Act (NTA) – consolidates CIT, PIT, PPT, VAT, CGT, Stamp Duties
  2. Nigeria Revenue Service Act – renames FIRS, strengthens autonomy
  3. Nigeria Tax Administration Act – uniform filing, audits, penalties
  4. Joint Revenue Board Act – harmonises FIRS and 36 states

Digital systems & enforcement

  • Mandatory e-filing & e-invoicing: VAT-registered businesses must use sequential, fiscalised invoices
  • TaxPro-Max 2.0: single portal for registration, filing, payment
  • Data matching: NIN-BVN-CAC-bank linking to detect undeclared income (digital assets, rent, POS)
  • Digital services: SEP now limited to e-commerce, streaming, data – taxed even without physical presence
  • Real-time reporting: large taxpayers submit monthly gross revenue data

Enforcement powers

  • Automatic bank liens (“substitution”) for unpaid tax
  • Tax clearance required for licences, contracts, international passports renewal (phased)
  • Penalties harmonised: late filing up to ₦100k individuals, higher for companies

Key rate changes

  • PIT: 0% on first ₦800k, top rate 25% above ₦50m
  • CIT: 0% for small companies (≤₦50m turnover, ≤₦250m assets), 30% others
  • CGT: no longer 10% – now aligned to PIT/CIT rates
  • Development Levy 4% replaces TET, NASENI, Police Trust Fund
