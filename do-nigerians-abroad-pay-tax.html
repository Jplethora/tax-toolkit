Diaspora questions

Do Nigerians living abroad still pay tax in Nigeria?

The answer depends less on passport status and more on what kind of income is involved, where it is sourced, and whether the individual is treated as resident for tax purposes.

Jerisma Plethora Team

This is one of the most common questions for diaspora Nigerians, especially those who still have rental property, business interests, or employers linked to Nigeria. The short version is that income connected to Nigeria may still require attention even when a person lives abroad.

Income that can still matter in Nigeria

  • Rental income from property located in Nigeria.
  • Business or professional income earned through Nigerian operations.
  • Employment income paid by a Nigerian employer in certain situations.
  • Gains connected to Nigerian assets.

Foreign income is a different question

Income earned entirely outside Nigeria may be treated differently from income that is sourced from Nigeria. That is why residency, source rules, and international overlap matter.

Double taxation concerns

People worry about paying twice on the same income. Depending on the countries involved, treaty rules or local relief mechanisms may shape that outcome.
