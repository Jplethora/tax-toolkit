Do Nigerians Living Abroad Pay Tax in Nigeria?

This is one of the most common questions asked by Nigerians in the diaspora who maintain financial connections to Nigeria.

The answer depends mainly on tax residency and source of income.

1. Nigerian Tax System

Nigeria generally taxes individuals based on residency and income source.

This means Nigerians living abroad may still have tax obligations if they earn income connected to Nigeria.

2. Income That May Be Taxable in Nigeria

  • Rental income from Nigerian property
  • Business income earned in Nigeria
  • Employment income paid by Nigerian employers
  • Capital gains from Nigerian assets

3. Foreign Income

Income earned entirely outside Nigeria is generally not taxed in Nigeria unless specific residency rules apply.

However, tax obligations may still exist in the country where the individual resides.

4. Double Taxation Agreements

Nigeria has tax treaties with several countries to avoid individuals being taxed twice on the same income.

These agreements help determine where taxes should be paid.

5. Compliance Awareness

Nigerians abroad who maintain investments, businesses, or property in Nigeria should understand how Nigerian tax rules apply to them.
