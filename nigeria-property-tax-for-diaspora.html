Property and diaspora

Property tax questions that often come up for diaspora Nigerians.

Owning property in Nigeria while living abroad can create tax questions around rental income, local charges, maintenance records, and eventual sale proceeds.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Diaspora property ownership is often emotional as well as financial. A house can be a family base, an investment, or both. That makes tax awareness especially important because the owner may not be physically present when records need attention.

Questions owners often need to ask

  • Is the property generating rental income that should be tracked?
  • Who is keeping records of payments, repairs, and occupancy?
  • Are local charges or taxes being handled consistently?
  • What will matter if the property is later sold?

Why records matter

For diaspora owners, clean records are often the difference between a calm review and a stressful reconstruction exercise. Keep tenancy agreements, receipts, statements, and transfer evidence together.

Cross-border reality: Distance does not remove the need for local documentation. It often increases the need for it.
