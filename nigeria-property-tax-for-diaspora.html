Do Nigerians Abroad Pay Tax on Property in Nigeria?

Many Nigerians living abroad invest in property in Nigeria for rental income, family use, or long-term investment.

Owning property in Nigeria can come with certain tax considerations depending on how the property is used.

1. Rental Income

If a property generates rental income, that income may fall under Nigerian personal income tax rules.

Examples include:

  • Residential rental income
  • Commercial property income
  • Short-term rental income

2. Capital Gains

If a property is sold at a profit, capital gains tax considerations may arise depending on the circumstances of the transaction.

3. Record Keeping

Diaspora property owners are encouraged to maintain records such as:

  • Property purchase documentation
  • Rental income records
  • Maintenance and management expenses
