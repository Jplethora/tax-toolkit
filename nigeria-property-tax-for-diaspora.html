Nigeria Property Tax for Diaspora Investors

Owning property in Nigeria while living abroad does not exempt you from tax. Under the Nigeria Tax Act 2025, non-residents are taxed on all Nigeria-sourced income.

Residency reminder

You are a Nigerian tax resident if you have a domicile, permanent home, habitual abode, or spend 183+ days in Nigeria in 12 months, or have strong economic/family ties. If not, you are non-resident – taxed only on Nigerian income.

What diaspora landlords must report

  • Rental income – gross rent less allowable expenses (repairs, agent fees, insurance, rates)
  • Withholding tax: tenants who are companies must deduct 10% WHT on rent and remit to FIRS/State IRS – claim as credit
  • Capital gains on sale – taxed at non-resident rates (individual bands up to 25%)
  • Indirect transfers – selling your foreign holding company that owns Lagos property is taxable in Nigeria

Record keeping checklist

  1. TIN linked to NIN (get via JTB portal from abroad)
  2. Tenancy agreements, rent receipts, bank statements
  3. Proof of expenses: agent commission, maintenance invoices, land use charge receipts
  4. Evidence of WHT deducted
  5. Property documents for cost base

Filing

  • File annual income tax return by March 31 via your property’s state eTax portal
  • Declare worldwide income only if you qualify as resident; otherwise just Nigerian rent
  • Appoint a tax representative in Nigeria for notices

Practical tips

  • Open a dedicated naira account for rent to simplify audit trail
  • Claim rent relief does NOT apply to landlords – it’s for tenants
  • Keep digital copies; FIRS now mandates e-invoicing for large landlords from 2026
  • Plan exit: use principal residence exemption only if property was your main home before relocating
