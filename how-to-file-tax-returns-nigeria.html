Filing tax returns

How tax returns are commonly approached in Nigeria.

This guide keeps the filing conversation practical: who may need to file, what to gather first, and where readers should slow down and get tailored help.

Jerisma Plethora Team

Built for Nigerian residents, freelancers, business owners, and diaspora families who want the topic explained plainly before they pay for help.

Filing tax returns can feel intimidating because it is often introduced late, after records are already messy. A better approach is to understand the filing process early, gather the right information, and then move step by step.

What many readers should prepare first

  • Basic identification details and any available tax number information.
  • Income records for the period in question.
  • Payroll summaries, invoices, or business records where relevant.
  • Evidence of deductible pension or qualifying expenses where they apply.

Who may need to pay closer attention

  • Self-employed individuals and freelancers.
  • Small-business owners.
  • People with multiple income streams.
  • Residents with cross-border income or complicated records.
Good habit: Treat filing as the last step of a record-keeping process, not the first time you look at your numbers.
