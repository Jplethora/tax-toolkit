Nigeria Tax Residency Rules Explained

Residency decides whether Nigeria taxes your worldwide income or only Nigerian income.

You are resident if ANY applies in a year

  1. Domicile: Nigeria is your permanent home you intend to return to
  2. Permanent home available: house/apartment for personal use (owned/rented)
  3. Habitual abode: you regularly live here
  4. 183-day rule: physical presence ≥183 days in any 12-month period (days need not be consecutive)
  5. Economic & family ties: substantial business interests in Nigeria, or spouse/dependants live here
  6. Diplomatic: Nigerian diplomats abroad remain resident

Tax treatment

  • Resident: taxed on worldwide income (Nigeria + abroad). Foreign tax credit available if DTA exists.
  • Non-resident: taxed only on income arising in or derived from Nigeria (salary for work in Nigeria, rent from Nigerian property, profit from Nigerian business, gains on Nigerian assets)

Common scenarios

  • Japa, no home/dependants in Nigeria, visit 60 days: Non-resident – only Nigerian rental income taxed
  • Remote worker for UK firm, family in Lagos, keeps flat: Resident – worldwide salary taxable in Nigeria (credit for UK tax)
  • Seafarer 200 days offshore, no Nigerian tie: Non-resident

Proof FIRS asks for

  • Passport stamps, flight tickets
  • Resident permits abroad
  • Utility bills, tenancy
  • Employment contracts

Tip: Keep a day-count diary. The 183-day test is easy to trigger.
