Do Freelancers Pay Tax in Nigeria?

The complete 2026 guide for Nigerian freelancers, creators, and remote workers on Personal Income Tax, filing, and staying compliant with FIRS & state IRS.

By Jerisma Editorial • 8 min read • Reviewed by Tax Partner

Nigeria's freelance economy has exploded. In 2026, over 1.5 million Nigerians earn full-time or side income online through platforms like Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, Deel, Payoneer, and direct foreign contracts. The short answer? Yes, you must pay tax.

Under the Personal Income Tax Act (PITA), all income earned by a person resident in Nigeria — whether from a Nigerian or foreign client, paid in naira, dollars, pounds, or crypto — is taxable. The FIRS and your State Internal Revenue Service (like LIRS, OIRS, etc.) have also increased monitoring of foreign inflows in 2025/2026.

This applies to all freelance income types, including:

  • Content writing, copywriting, and ghostwriting
  • Graphic design, UI/UX, and video editing
  • Software development, web design, and no-code builds
  • Virtual assistance, customer support, and admin work
  • Social media management, ads, and digital marketing
  • Consulting, coaching, tutoring, and online courses
  • YouTube, TikTok, podcasting, and UGC sponsorships
  • Affiliate marketing, dropshipping, and e-commerce

1 Tax Obligations for Freelancers

As a freelancer, you are classified as "self-employed" and are taxed under Personal Income Tax, not Company Income Tax. This means you:

  • Must obtain a Tax Identification Number (TIN) from the Joint Tax Board (JTB)
  • Register with your state IRS (e.g., LIRS for Lagos residents)
  • File annual tax returns via self-assessment — even if clients don't deduct tax
  • Pay tax on your worldwide income if you are resident in Nigeria for more than 183 days

Good news: the 2026 tax bands give you a ₦800,000 tax-free allowance, then 15% to 25% on the balance. You can also deduct legitimate business expenses before tax is calculated.

2 Which Income Sources Are Taxable?

All of them. The tax authority doesn't care if the money came from Upwork or a direct wire from the UK. If it's payment for your work, it's taxable when you receive it in Nigeria.

  • Platform payouts: Upwork, Fiverr, Freelancer.com, Toptal (via Payoneer, Wise, PayPal)
  • Direct clients: USD, GBP, EUR wire transfers or crypto conversions
  • Creator income: YouTube AdSense, TikTok Creator Fund, brand sponsorships
  • Digital products: Gumroad, Paystack storefronts, course sales

Important: Convert foreign income using the CBN rate on the day you receive it. Keep your Payoneer/Wise statements — LIRS now requests them during audits.

3 Record Keeping: What to Track

Your tax bill is calculated on profit, not total revenue. Good records can legally save you hundreds of thousands annually.

  • Save every invoice and contract (PDF in Google Drive)
  • Keep all bank, Payoneer, Wise, and Grey statements monthly
  • Track deductible expenses: laptop & phone, data subscription, NEPA/inverter, coworking space, software (Canva, Adobe, GitHub, VPN), online courses, and 10% of rent if you work from home
  • Use a separate business account — don't mix with personal spending
  • Use a simple spreadsheet or Wave/QuickBooks Self-Employed

4 Understanding Tax Compliance

Compliance is simple once you set it up. The deadline for filing your previous year's income is March 31st every year.

  1. Get your TIN at tin.jtb.gov.ng (free, 5 minutes)
  2. Register on your state's e-tax portal and file annual returns
  3. Pay via Remita and download your payment receipt
  4. Request your Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC) — needed for visas, loans, government contracts

Late filing penalty is ₦5,000 + ₦100 per day in Lagos and most states. Filing on time also helps you avoid issues when banks request proof of income for mortgages or when applying for a UK/Canada visa.
