Personal Income Tax in Nigeria Explained

Personal Income Tax (PIT) is now in Chapter 2 of the Nigeria Tax Act 2025. It applies to all individuals earning income connected to Nigeria.

What is taxable

  • Employment: salary, wages, bonuses, allowances, benefits-in-kind
  • Business/profession: trade profits
  • Investment: dividends, interest, rent, royalties
  • Capital gains: property, shares, crypto, digital assets
  • Other: prizes, winnings, honoraria, grants

What is exempt

  • First ₦800,000 of chargeable income (0% band)
  • Minimum wage earners
  • Pensions under Pension Reform Act, gratuities, armed forces salaries
  • Agricultural income – first 5 years
  • Export proceeds repatriated
  • Compensation for loss of employment up to ₦50m

Deductions you can claim

  • Pension contributions
  • NHF contributions
  • NHIS contributions
  • Life insurance premiums
  • Interest on owner-occupied housing loan
  • Rent relief: 20% of rent, max ₦500,000 per year

Example

Earn ₦6m salary, pay ₦300k pension, ₦2m rent. Deductions = ₦300k + ₦400k (20% of rent capped at 500k) = ₦700k. Chargeable = ₦5.3m. Tax ≈ ₦744k (first 800k free, next 2.2m @15% = 330k, balance 2.3m @18% = 414k).

File by March 31.
