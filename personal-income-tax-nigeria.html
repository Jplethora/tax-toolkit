Personal Income Tax in Nigeria Explained

A basic guide to how individual taxation works in Nigeria

What Is Personal Income Tax?

Personal Income Tax (PIT) is the tax charged on income earned by individuals in Nigeria. It applies to employment income, business income, professional income, and certain investment earnings.

---

Who May Be Required to Pay Personal Income Tax?

Individuals who earn income through employment, professional services, or business activities may have tax obligations depending on their income level and residency status.

---

Types of Income That May Be Taxable

Examples include:
  • Salary and wages
  • Professional service income
  • Business profits
  • Certain investment income
  • Rental income from property
---

Employees vs Self-Employed Individuals

Employees typically have tax deducted from their salary through the Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) system managed by employers.

Self-employed individuals, freelancers, and business owners may be responsible for calculating and filing their own tax returns.
