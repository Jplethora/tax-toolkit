How to Calculate PAYE Tax in Nigeria

A simplified guide to Pay-As-You-Earn tax calculation

What Is PAYE?

PAYE (Pay-As-You-Earn) is the system used in Nigeria to deduct personal income tax directly from employees’ salaries. Employers calculate and remit the tax to the relevant state tax authority.

PAYE deductions are part of the Nigerian personal income tax system. Learn more about Personal Income Tax in Nigeria.

Basic Steps in PAYE Calculation

PAYE calculation generally involves the following steps:

  1. Determine gross annual income
  2. Apply statutory deductions (such as pension contributions)
  3. Apply Consolidated Relief Allowance
  4. Calculate taxable income
  5. Apply the Nigerian tax bands
---

Nigeria Personal Income Tax Bands

Income Band Tax Rate
First ₦300,000 7%
Next ₦300,000 11%
Next ₦500,000 15%
Next ₦500,000 19%
Next ₦1,600,000 21%
Above ₦3,200,000 24%
---

These tax bands form the basis of how PAYE deductions are calculated. You can see a detailed explanation here: Nigeria Tax Rates Explained.

Example PAYE Calculation

If an employee earns ₦2,400,000 annually, PAYE tax would be calculated after applying relevant reliefs and deductions before applying the tax bands.
