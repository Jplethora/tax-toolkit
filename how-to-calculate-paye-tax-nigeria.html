How to Calculate PAYE Tax in Nigeria

Simple Breakdown for 2026

Pay As You Earn (PAYE) is the system through which Federal and State governments in Nigeria collect Personal Income Tax directly from employees' salaries. Your employer is legally required to deduct the correct tax every month and remit it to your state's Internal Revenue Service (IRS). Understanding how this calculation works in 2026 helps you verify your payslip, avoid over-taxation, and plan your finances accurately.

Basic Steps in PAYE Calculation

PAYE is not charged as a flat rate. It is calculated progressively on your taxable income after all reliefs. Follow these steps:

  1. Determine Gross Annual Income: Add your basic salary, housing, transport, leave allowance, 13th month, and all other bonuses.
  2. Deduct Statutory Contributions: Subtract allowable reliefs like Pension (8% of gross), NHF (2.5% of basic), and NHIS contributions.
  3. Apply Consolidated Relief Allowance (CRA): Under the 2026 Finance Act, you are granted a tax-free allowance of ₦800,000 plus 20% of your gross income.
  4. Calculate Taxable Income: Gross Income minus (Statutory Deductions + CRA) = Your Taxable Income.
  5. Apply Tax Bands Progressively: Use the 2026 bands below. You only pay the higher rate on the portion of income that falls into that band.

Nigeria Personal Income Tax Bands 2026

The new simplified 2026 tax schedule replaced the old 7-band system. Here are the correct rates to use:

Taxable Income Band Rate
First ₦800,000 0%
Next ₦2,200,000 15%
Next ₦9,000,000 18%
Next ₦13,000,000 21%
Next ₦25,000,000 23%
Above ₦50,000,000 25%

Example PAYE Calculation

Let's use a staff earning ₦6,000,000 per annum (₦500,000/month):

  • Gross Income: ₦6,000,000
  • Less: Pension (8%) + NHF: - ₦630,000
  • Less: CRA (₦800k + 20%): - ₦2,000,000
  • Taxable Income: ₦3,370,000
  • Tax: First ₦800k @ 0%: ₦0
  • Next ₦2,200k @ 15%: ₦330,000
  • Balance ₦370k @ 18%: ₦66,600
  • Annual PAYE: ₦396,600
  • Monthly PAYE: ≈ ₦33,050

Importance of Accurate Payroll Records

Keeping accurate payslips and annual tax deduction cards (Form H1) is critical. It helps you file your annual tax returns, apply for loans, process visas, and prove tax compliance. Employers who fail to remit PAYE correctly face penalties of 10% annual interest plus the unpaid tax. Always cross-check your deductions monthly.

Tax Identification Number

To be properly captured in the PAYE system, every employee in Nigeria needs a Tax Identification Number (TIN). Your TIN links all your tax payments across states and is required for your Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC). You can get your TIN for free online via the FIRS or your State IRS portal in under 10 minutes.

Educational Purpose Only

This guide is based on the 2026 Nigerian Finance Act and is for educational purposes. Tax reliefs and state rates may vary slightly. Always consult your HR, payroll provider, or a certified tax professional for your exact computation.
