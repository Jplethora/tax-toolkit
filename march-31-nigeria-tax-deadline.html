Who Must File Tax Returns Before March 31?

  • Salaried employees (PAYE workers)
  • Freelancers and remote workers
  • Business owners (sole proprietors)
  • Individuals with multiple income streams
  • Landlords and investors

Even if you earned little or nothing, you may still be required to file a NIL return under Nigerian tax regulations.

---

Important: PAYE Does Not Replace Filing

Many employees believe that once tax is deducted from their salary, no further action is required.

However, filing your annual tax return remains a legal requirement in Nigeria.

---

What Happens If You Miss the March 31 Deadline?

  • Penalties and fines may apply
  • You may receive compliance notices
  • Difficulty obtaining Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC)
  • Possible additional tax assessments
---

March vs April: What You Should Know

March is the compliance period for filing returns. April is typically when enforcement actions begin.

Filing early reduces the risk of errors, penalties, and delays.

---

How to Prepare Before Filing Your Tax Return

  • Review all income sources (salary, freelance, rent, investments)
  • Gather supporting documents
  • Ensure you have a valid Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • Understand applicable tax reliefs and deductions
