March 31 Tax Deadline Nigeria (2026)

Nigeria's personal income tax filing season now runs under the Nigeria Tax Act 2025, effective January 1, 2026. The key date for individuals is March 31.

Who must file by March 31, 2026

  • Self-employed, freelancers, professionals, traders
  • Business owners (sole proprietors, partnerships)
  • Employees with other income (rent, dividends, crypto, side hustle)
  • Directors earning fees outside PAYE
  • Non-residents with Nigeria-sourced income

PAYE-only employees are filed by employers, but you must still file if you have additional income.

Key deadlines

  • January 31: Employers file annual PAYE returns (Form H1)
  • March 31: Individuals file 2025 personal income tax returns

Penalties for late filing

  • Up to ₦100,000 for individuals, plus monthly interest
  • Enforcement includes “power of substitution” (bank account liens)
  • Non-filing blocks tax clearance certificates (TCC)

How to prepare

  1. Get your TIN – how to get TIN
  2. Gather records: bank statements, rent receipts, invoices, expense proofs, crypto trades
  3. Calculate total income 2025 (employment + business + investment + capital gains)
  4. Claim deductions: pension, NHF, NHIS, life insurance, rent relief (20% of rent up to ₦500k)
  5. File via your State IRS eTax portal or FIRS TaxPro-Max (for FCT residents)

What’s new in 2026

  • All returns electronic – manual filing phased out in most states
  • Capital gains now taxed at PIT bands (up to 25%), not flat 10%
  • Digital assets, prizes, honoraria are taxable
  • Minimum wage earners (₦800k first band at 0%) effectively exempt
